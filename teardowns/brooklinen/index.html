Key findings

Pricing landscape

Snowe’s sheet sets run $123.50–$270 for full sets and duvets, with linen pillow cases at a consistent $90. Coyuchi’s extracted pricing focuses on sleepwear at $98–$128 and renewed towels at $35–$75. Brooklinen occupies a broad mid-premium band that overlaps both competitors in the sheet and bedding accessory space.

Catalog positioning

Snowe emphasizes classic percale and linen bedding with final-sale pricing on blush colorways. Coyuchi leans into organic cotton sleepwear and renewed (discounted) towel sets. Neither competitor publishes their core sheet-set pricing as aggressively on landing pages as Brooklinen does — a merchandising difference, not a price gap.

Demand signals

Snowe’s linen pillow cases are marked “Selling Out Soon” at $90, and Coyuchi’s renewed Mediterranean Organic Towels at $75 suggest strong demand in the sustainable-luxury towel segment. Both are categories where Brooklinen could test price elasticity or bundle positioning.
