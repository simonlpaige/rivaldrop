What we capture

Six signals that actually move a Shopify brand's numbers.

Most "competitive intel" tools watch press releases and SEO. We watch the things that change a buyer's decision in the next two weeks.

Price

Daily price moves on the rival's bestseller.

Not their full catalog. The specific SKU that is eating into your conversion. Flagged within 24 hours.

Restock

Stock-out and restock pulses.

When the rival sells through, you see it the same day. That is when their email list is most likely to look at yours.

Bestseller

Catalog rank movement.

Which of their products climbed, which fell. Tells you what their customers are actually buying this month.

New entrant

New product launches.

Drop pages, new collection URLs, Kickstarter or community launches that touch your category.

Reputation

Reddit, Trustpilot, and forum mentions.

The signal you cannot get from a tool that only watches their site. People talk before they buy.

Roadmap

Monthly category gap read.

Three product or content gaps in your category, ranked by momentum. Useful when you are deciding what to ship next.

A real example

What a Monday brief looks like for a Shopify brand.

Three or four bullets. Each one names a rival, a number, and a date. No takes. We tell you what we saw and what we do not yet know.

A typical Monday brief takes about four minutes to read. It is the email you skim before your team standup, not a dashboard you have to log into.
