How it works

How does rivalDrop actually work?

Five short sections. The first three are what you get. The fourth is what you don't. The fifth is how to start.

Phase 01 · The morning brief

What lands in your inbox before 7am.

The morning brief is an email. Not a PDF, not a portal. It opens with a number or a question. Underneath: three or four bullets. Each bullet leads with a rival's name. Each one cites a date and a piece of evidence. The whole thing takes about four minutes to read.

The brief sends Monday through Friday. We don't send on weekends because nothing useful happens on weekends in B2B and we'd rather not pretend otherwise. If something hot surfaces on a Saturday, you'll see it Monday at 7am, marked with a HOT label, ahead of everything else.

A typical week contains 14 to 22 captured signals. Most are quiet. Two or three are worth bringing into a sales call.

Phase 02 · The watchlist

How rivals get added and how signals get captured.

You start with three rivals. We agree on those during a 15-minute setup call. Adding a fourth or fifth is included for the first 60 days, no extra charge. After that, additions are case by case.

For each rival, we set up a watch on the public surfaces that matter: pricing pages, careers pages, product pages, blog posts, ad libraries, leadership pages, and a small set of secondary sources we've found useful (case studies, partner directories, status pages, podcast appearances).

About 80% of signal capture is automated. The other 20% is a human review pass: someone reads the captured changes and decides what to surface. Reading is the part you can't skip. A pricing page can change because they renamed a tier; that's not a signal. A pricing page can change because they pulled a discount that was anchoring half their pipeline; that's a signal. The difference is the part we won't automate away.

Phase 03 · The dashboard

The live web view, when the brief isn't enough.

Most days, the email is the whole product. The dashboard is for the days you need to dig. It's a single web page per account, filterable three ways: by rival, by signal type, by date. Every captured signal links to the source we saw it on, with the date we saw it, and a short note about why we surfaced it.

Two specific moments people use the dashboard:

  • Before a competitive bake-off, to pull a one-page dossier on the rival in question. We'll generate that for you on request, usually inside two business hours.
  • After a deal is lost, to look back at the 30 days before it closed and ask which signals we missed. Sometimes we missed nothing. Sometimes we missed something. Either way, knowing changes the next deal.
Phase 04 · What we don't do

The honest list of limits.

Three things rivalDrop will not do for you, on purpose.

We don't make competitive recommendations. We tell you what changed and what we think it might mean. We don't tell you what to bid in your next deal or what to put in your next deck. That's your judgement. We're trying to give you better raw material, not replace the call.

We don't sell the underlying data. What we capture for your watchlist stays for your watchlist. We don't aggregate clients' watchlists into a market intelligence product. If we ever did, you'd be the first to know and the first to opt out.

We don't replace your sales process. If your problem is that nobody's prospecting or you can't get champions to call back, more rival intel won't fix it. The brief makes a good sales process sharper. It doesn't make a missing one appear.

One more honest thing: we miss things. Some of what rivals do is private until it isn't. We'll always tell you what we couldn't see.

Phase 05 · What's next

How to start.

Fifteen-minute setup. We'll ask who your top three rivals are, what you're trying to learn, and which deals or moments are coming up. The first brief lands within five business days.

If we can't capture useful signal in week one, you don't pay for week two. That's the deal.
