What we watch

Sales is a chess endgame. Most pieces are off the board, every move counts more.

By the time a deal reaches the renewal or the bake-off, you already know the rivals on the board. There are usually three. They make moves between calls. You react to those moves whether you can see them or not.

rivalDrop watches the interaction between you and a known set of rivals, and captures the signal that comes out of the collision. Pricing changes. Hiring posts. New product pages. Champion shifts. The small things that, taken together, tell you what's about to happen on the next call.

Most of what we capture is small. That's the point. Big news travels on its own.

Signals we capture

What a real brief sounds like.

Three or four bullets, never more. Each one names a rival, a number, and a date. No takes. No takes pretending to be facts. We tell you what we saw and what we don't yet know.

Pricing

Acme raised prices 12% on Tuesday.

Their two largest customers renew in Q3. Worth a call. If you have a price-lock clause, dig it out today.

Hiring

Beacon posted three roles for "channel-led" reps.

First time the phrase shows up on their careers page. The direct sales team is being repositioned. We don't yet know who's leading it.

Product

Cogent quietly shipped a new tier called Pro Plus.

$1,200 a seat. It sits between their old Pro and Enterprise prices. Three of your open deals were considering Pro this week.

Who it's for

Who is this built for?

Sales teams in mid-market and SMB B2B. Anyone who sells against a known set of rivals and would rather hear a small thing on Tuesday than a big thing on Friday.

Three roles get the most out of it:

  • Account executives prepping for renewals, ramps, or expansion calls.
  • Sales leaders running competitive deals where the rival list is short and the dollar value is long.
  • Founders selling against funded competitors who keep changing the goalposts.

If you sell to a wide unknown universe, this isn't the right tool. Watch works because the watchlist is small enough to be precise.
